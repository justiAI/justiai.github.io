Access to justice is a systems problem

Procedural complexity Formal processes can be hard to navigate for people with limited legal literacy.
Institutional barriers Support systems exist, but users often do not know where to start or what to prepare.
Need for navigation Users need action-oriented procedural guidance, not abstract legal analysis.
Safer legal-tech JustiAI reduces friction while avoiding automated legal advice.

User scenarios

Migrant worker Ana needs unpaid wage help Ana is unsure whether to call a hotline, contact the employer, or apply for mediation. JustiAI helps her organize wage records and identify the labor authority route.
Part-time worker Ming faces overtime confusion Ming has screenshots of shifts and salary slips but does not know what counts as useful evidence. JustiAI turns the situation into a checklist and next-step plan.

Simplifying what feels impossibly complex

Step-by-step guidance Breaks procedures into simple actions, required documents, and next steps.
Navigation, not advice Provides procedural direction while avoiding legal interpretation and outcome prediction.
Inclusive design Supports Chinese and English experiences for first-time users and foreign residents.
Institutional linkage Connects users with labor authorities, hotlines, and legal aid resources.

Strategic positioning

Strengths

What we do well

  • Focuses on procedural navigation rather than legal advice.
  • Modular design enables expansion across legal domains.
  • Bilingual access supports a broader user group.
Weaknesses

Where we need work

  • Requires continuous updates and validation.
  • Must build trust with vulnerable user groups.
  • Limited early monetization potential.
Opportunities

Paths to grow

  • Aligns with Taiwan's digital governance initiatives.
  • Potential partnerships with government bodies and NGOs.
  • Few pure procedural navigation tools in legal-tech.
Threats

Risks to watch

  • Regulatory scrutiny and privacy concerns.
  • Competition from established technology firms.
  • Users may misunderstand AI outputs without clear boundaries.

      Procedural knowledge base

      Labor mediation Organize facts, submit an application, attend mediation, and keep the written record.
      Complaint or inspection Use when wages, overtime, working time, or safety issues require labor authority review.
      Legal aid referral Escalate when settlement fails, documents are complex, or court action may be needed.
      Migrant worker support Use worker hotlines and public agencies when language, employer control, or housing concerns affect access.

      Legal-safety boundaries

      Allowed output
      • Explain public procedures and required documents.
      • Suggest institutions that can receive complaints or provide help.
      • Turn a user situation into a neutral preparation checklist.
      • Display official links and verification reminders.
      Blocked output
      • No legal opinion about who is right or wrong.
      • No prediction of settlement amount or court outcome.
      • No instruction to ignore official notices or deadlines.
      • No replacement for lawyers, NGOs, courts, or labor authorities.
